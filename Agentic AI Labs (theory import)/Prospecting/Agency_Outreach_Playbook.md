1. WHY AGENCIES ARE THE HIGHEST-LEVERAGE PLAY
================================================================

One direct client = one project.
One agency partner = 3-5 projects per year. Every year.

The math is simple:

  Direct outreach to founders:
    You find 1 client → You deliver 1 project → You start over.
    You do ALL the work: finding, pitching, selling, building.

  Agency partnership:
    You find 1 agency → They sell to THEIR clients → You build.
    They do the finding. They do the selling. You just build.
    One agency with 20 clients = 3-5 AI projects/year without you
    doing any outbound.

  5 agency partners x 4 projects/year = 20 projects/year.
  You did outreach to 5 people. Not 20.

This is the Rockefeller "secret allies" strategy:

  Rockefeller didn't sell kerosene to every household himself.
  He controlled the refining, then let distributors handle the
  last mile. The distributors needed him more than he needed them —
  because without his refining capacity, they had nothing to sell.

  You are the refiner. The agency is the distributor. Without your
  technical capability, they can't deliver the AI services they're
  already promising to their clients.


WHY NOW

GHL agencies are in a specific moment right now:

  - AI is the hottest upsell in the agency world
  - Every agency owner sees competitors offering "AI-powered" services
  - They're under pressure to add AI to their menu or lose clients
  - But 90% of them are marketers, not builders
  - They can set up a GHL workflow. They cannot build a production
    voice agent connected to memory and CRM automation.
  - They're stuck between "I need to sell AI" and "I can't build AI"

You walk into this gap. That's why this works.

2. UNDERSTANDING THE AGENCY OWNER'S WORLD
================================================================

Before you DM a single agency owner, you need to understand their
business, their psychology, and their daily reality.


WHO THEY ARE

  - Typically a solo founder or small team (1-5 people)
  - Marketing background, not technical
  - Runs on GHL (GoHighLevel) as their primary platform
  - Serves local businesses: dental, real estate, fitness, home services,
    med spas, chiropractors, lawyers, HVAC
  - Revenue model: monthly retainers ($500-$3,000/month per client)
    + one-time setup fees ($1,000-$5,000)
  - Has 10-50 clients at any given time
  - Always looking for ways to increase revenue per client and reduce churn


WHAT THEIR DAY LOOKS LIKE

  - Managing client campaigns (ads, funnels, email sequences)
  - Handling client questions and requests
  - Trying to reduce churn (clients leave when they don't see results)
  - Looking for the next service to upsell (AI is top of mind right now)
  - Scrolling GHL Facebook groups for ideas, templates, workflows
  - Watching what other agency owners are doing and copying what works


THEIR PAIN (THE THING THAT MAKES THEM LISTEN TO YOU)

  Pain 1: "My clients are asking for AI and I don't know how to deliver."
    Clients see AI everywhere. They ask their agency "can you do this?"
    The agency owner says "yes" and then panics.

  Pain 2: "I tried to build it myself and it's not production-ready."
    They've played with GHL's built-in chatbot. Maybe tried Vapi.
    It works as a demo. It breaks with real customers. They can't
    charge $2,000/month for something that embarrasses their client.

  Pain 3: "My competitors are offering AI services and I'm falling behind."
    They see other agencies in the GHL groups posting about AI voice
    agents, AI appointment setters, AI lead qualification. They feel
    the pressure. If they don't offer it, clients will go to someone
    who does.

  Pain 4: "I want recurring revenue, not just one-time setups."
    AI services can be sold as monthly retainers ($500-$2,000/month
    on top of existing marketing retainer). This is pure profit
    expansion. But only if the AI actually works reliably enough
    to justify ongoing fees.


WHAT THEY WANT (THE THING THAT MAKES THEM SAY YES)

  - A technical partner who builds, so they can sell
  - White-labeled — their brand, not yours
  - Reliable — it has to work with real customers, not just demos
  - Repeatable — a system they can sell to multiple clients, not
    a custom build every time
  - Simple handoff — they sell it, introduce you to the client's
    situation, you build it, they deliver it
  - Margin — they need to charge retail and pay you wholesale,
    keeping 30-50% margin


WHAT THEY FEAR

  - That you'll go directly to their clients and cut them out
  - That the AI will break and make THEM look bad
  - That it's too expensive for their clients to afford
  - That it's too complex for them to sell confidently
  - That you'll be slow, unresponsive, or unreliable

3. THE TWO TYPES OF AGENCY PARTNERS
================================================================

Not all agencies are the same. There are two distinct types, and
you approach each differently.


TYPE A: "ALREADY SELLING AI" AGENCY

  Who they are:
    Already marketing AI services (voice agents, chatbots, AI
    appointment setters) to their clients. Have it on their website
    or are posting about it in GHL groups.

  Their situation:
    They've sold the promise. Now they need to deliver. Some are
    building it themselves (poorly). Some have tried other technical
    partners (and been burned). Some are faking it with basic
    GHL chatbot features.

  Your angle:
    "I see you're already offering AI to your clients. Are you
    building it yourself or working with a technical team?"
    This probes whether they're struggling to deliver.

  Why they say yes:
    They NEED you. They've already sold the service. They just
    need someone who can actually build it. You're solving an
    immediate, urgent problem.

  Timeline to close: Fast. 1-2 weeks. They have active demand.

  Risk: They may have bad client expectations already set.
    You'll need to reset what's realistic on the first project.


TYPE B: "THINKING ABOUT AI" AGENCY

  Who they are:
    Haven't started selling AI yet. But they see it everywhere.
    They're in the GHL groups asking questions like "has anyone
    added AI voice agents to their agency?" or "what's the best
    way to offer AI to clients?"

  Their situation:
    Curious but hesitant. They don't know what's possible, how
    much to charge, or how to deliver. They need someone to show
    them the whole picture — the product, the pricing, the pitch
    to their own clients.

  Your angle:
    "I noticed you're exploring AI for your agency. We work with
    agencies as their technical backend — we build the AI, you
    sell it under your brand. Want to see how it works?"

  Why they say yes:
    You're not just offering to build — you're offering a turnkey
    revenue stream. You show them what to sell, how to price it,
    and you build it for them. You're reducing their risk to zero.

  Timeline to close: Slower. 3-4 weeks. They need education first.

  Advantage: You shape expectations from the start. No cleanup.

5. THE WARM-UP PHASE (WEEKS 1-3)
================================================================

Rockefeller's rule: "Trapped the prey before the seduction had begun."
You never DM an agency owner cold. Here's the warm-up.


WEEK 1: OBSERVE AND LEARN

  - Join 3-5 GHL Facebook groups
  - Read 20-30 posts per day. Don't post. Don't comment.
  - Learn their language. Understand:
    - What tools they use (GHL, Twilio, Vapi, ChatGPT)
    - What they call things ("AI appointment setter," "speed to lead
      bot," "AI receptionist," "conversational AI")
    - What they struggle with (latency, accuracy, CRM integration,
      client onboarding)
    - Who the respected voices are in the group
    - Which agency owners are active and engaged
  - Start a tracking list: Name | Agency | What They Sell |
    AI Status (selling/exploring/none) | Where Found | Notes


WEEK 2: BECOME VISIBLE THROUGH VALUE

  Now you start contributing. But you ONLY help. You never pitch.

  What to do:
    - Answer 3-5 questions per week with genuinely useful answers
    - Focus on questions about AI, voice agents, automation, CRM
      integration
    - Give detailed, specific answers. Not "you should try Vapi"
      but "here's how we connected a voice agent to a GHL workflow:
      [specific steps, maybe a screenshot or short video]"

  Content to share (proactively, not as a response):
    - Short walkthroughs: "Built this AI appointment setter that
      plugs into GHL. Books appointments, updates contacts, triggers
      follow-ups. 60-second demo: [link]"
    - Mini case study: "Set up an AI voice agent for a dental
      practice through a GHL workflow. It handles 200+ calls/month
      now. Here's the basic architecture."
    - Helpful tip: "If your GHL chatbot is dropping conversations,
      here's what's usually happening and how to fix it..."

  The goal: By end of week 2, 10-15 people in the group recognize
  your name as "the AI person who actually knows what they're doing."


WEEK 3: IDENTIFY AND DM

  By now you should have:
    - 5-10 agency owners on your tracking list who are either
      already selling AI (Type A) or actively exploring (Type B)
    - Visibility in the group from your helpful posts/answers
    - At least 1-2 direct interactions with target agency owners
      (you answered their question, they liked your post, etc.)

  Now you DM. See Section 7 for the specific sequences.

6. IDENTIFYING THE RIGHT AGENCY PARTNER (SIGNALS)
================================================================

Not every agency owner is worth your time. Look for these signals.


GREEN SIGNALS (REACH OUT)

  - They're actively posting about AI services in the group
  - They're asking technical questions about voice agents or
    AI integration (means they're trying and struggling)
  - They have a website that mentions "AI" as a service
  - They have 15+ clients (enough volume to generate multiple
    AI projects per year)
  - They respond to other people's posts about AI (means they
    care about the topic)
  - They're running a GHL SaaS Mode setup (more sophisticated,
    likely thinking about scale)
  - They serve industries where AI has clear ROI: dental, real
    estate, home services, med spas, fitness


YELLOW SIGNALS (PROCEED WITH CAUTION)

  - Very small agency (fewer than 5 clients) — they might not
    generate enough volume to justify the partnership effort
  - They're focused on very low-ticket clients — their clients
    may not be able to afford AI add-ons
  - They seem to change their offerings every month — they might
    not commit to AI long enough to make the partnership work
  - They're very price-sensitive in their group posts — they'll
    likely push back hard on your pricing


RED SIGNALS (AVOID — SEE SECTION 15)

  - They claim to have already "built" advanced AI but their
    demos look basic — ego problem, hard to partner with
  - They're posting negative things about agencies who outsource
    technical work — they'll be resistant to the white-label model
  - They have a history of hopping between different services
    every few weeks — they won't commit
  - They're in groups mainly to sell their own stuff, not to
    learn — they won't value your expertise

7. THE DM SEQUENCES
================================================================

Two different sequences for the two types of agencies.


SEQUENCE A: FOR "ALREADY SELLING AI" AGENCIES
(They have the demand. They need the delivery.)

  DM 1 — The Opener:

    Hey [Name],

    Been seeing your posts about [specific thing — their AI
    service, a workflow they shared, a question they asked].
    Looks like you're ahead of most agencies on the AI side.

    Quick question — are you building the AI layer in-house
    or working with a technical team for that?

  Why this works:
    - Specific reference (not copy-paste)
    - Compliment that positions them as ahead (ego stroke)
    - The question probes the exact pain point: delivery

  If they say "building it myself":

    Nice. What stack are you using? We're a 3-person AI studio
    and we build the voice + CRM + automation layer for agencies
    as a white-label backend. Most agency owners we talk to have
    the GHL side dialed but hit a wall on the voice agent +
    memory piece.

    Happy to show you a quick demo of what the full system
    looks like if you're curious. No pitch — just want to
    see if there's a fit.

  If they say "looking for someone" / "tried someone and it didn't work":

    Got it. We might be exactly what you need.

    We're a 3-person AI systems studio. We build the full
    stack — voice agent, memory (so the AI remembers every
    caller), and CRM automation — all plugged into GHL.
    White-labeled under your brand. Your clients never know
    we exist.

    I can show you a working demo in 5 minutes — you can
    literally call a phone number and talk to the AI.
    Want me to send it over?

  If they say "doing it with GHL's built-in tools":

    Smart — GHL's native stuff works for basic use cases.

    Where we usually come in is when agencies want to go
    beyond the built-in chatbot — production voice agents
    that handle real calls, memory that remembers returning
    customers, and deeper CRM automation (auto-qualification,
    smart follow-ups, etc.).

    If you ever want to see what that looks like, happy to
    show you a demo. Might give you ideas for your next
    client upsell.


SEQUENCE B: FOR "THINKING ABOUT AI" AGENCIES
(They're curious. They need the full picture.)

  DM 1 — The Opener:

    Hey [Name],

    Saw your [post/question] about adding AI to your agency
    services. It's the right move — every agency we talk to
    is thinking about it right now.

    Quick question: are you looking to build it yourself
    or would you rather have a technical team handle the
    AI build while you focus on selling?

  If they say "not sure yet" / "exploring options":

    Totally fair. Here's how most agencies we work with
    approach it:

    They sell "AI-powered [appointment setting / customer
    support / lead qualification]" to their clients. We
    build the AI system behind it — voice agents, memory,
    CRM automation — all white-labeled under their brand.

    The agency charges the client $1,500-$3,000/month for
    the AI service. They pay us a wholesale rate. They keep
    the margin.

    I can walk you through exactly how this works — the
    product, the pricing, even how to pitch it to your
    clients. Want to jump on a quick 15-minute call?

  Why this works for Type B:
    - You're not just offering to build — you're offering
      a business model
    - You told them what to charge, what the margin looks
      like, and that you'll even help them sell it
    - You've reduced their risk to near zero
    - The 15-minute call is low commitment


FOLLOW-UP RULES (BOTH SEQUENCES)

  If no response after 48 hours:
    - Don't follow up immediately
    - Engage with one of their posts in the group (like, comment)
    - Wait 5-7 days

  Follow-up DM (after 5-7 days):

    Hey [Name], no worries if the timing's off. Just dropped
    a quick demo of an AI voice agent we built for a
    [dental/real estate/fitness] client — [link to Loom or
    live demo number].

    Figured it might spark some ideas for your agency
    regardless. Happy to chat whenever.

  If no response after follow-up:
    - Move on. Do NOT send a third message.
    - Continue being visible in the group. They'll come back
      when they're ready.

  Maximum DMs to any one person: 2. Ever.

8. THE PITCH: WHAT THE AGENCY ACTUALLY BUYS
================================================================

The agency does NOT buy "AI systems." They buy a revenue stream.

Here's how to frame it at each stage of the conversation.


WHAT YOU SAY IN THE DM (GETTING THE CALL)

  You sell. We build. White-labeled under your brand.
  Your clients never know we exist.

  This is enough to get the call. Don't over-explain in DMs.


WHAT YOU SAY ON THE CALL (THE PARTNERSHIP PITCH)

  Open with their world, not yours:

    "Most agencies right now are in one of two spots: they're
    either already selling AI and struggling to deliver, or
    they know they need to add AI but don't know where to start.
    Which one sounds more like you?"

  Then, depending on their answer, show them the product
  through the lens of THEIR business:

    "Here's what we build for agencies like yours.

    Let's say you have a dental client. Right now, that dental
    practice has a front desk person answering calls, booking
    appointments, handling rescheduling, answering insurance
    questions. That's 6 hours a day of manual work.

    We build an AI receptionist for that practice. It picks up
    every call — no hold times, no missed calls. It books
    appointments directly into their calendar. It remembers
    returning patients. It sends confirmations and reminders.
    24/7.

    You sell that to your dental client as an add-on to their
    marketing retainer. You charge them $1,500-$2,500/month.
    You pay us a wholesale rate. You keep the margin.

    Your client gets an AI receptionist that works. You get
    recurring revenue without building anything. We build
    and maintain the system.

    Want to hear what it sounds like? Call this number."

  Then let them call the demo. The demo sells itself.


THE AI ROLES FOR AGENCY CLIENTS (WHAT THE AGENCY SELLS)

The agency doesn't sell "voice + memory + automation" to their end
client. They sell an AI Role — a specific AI employee. Here are the
most common ones for GHL agency client industries:

  Industry: Dental / Medical / Clinics
  AI Role: AI Receptionist
  What it does: Answers every call, books appointments, remembers
    patients, sends confirmations, handles rescheduling
  What the agency charges: $1,500-$2,500/month
  Pitch the agency gives their client: "Your AI receptionist handles
    every call 24/7. No more missed calls. No more hold times."

  Industry: Real Estate
  AI Role: AI Showing Coordinator
  What it does: Answers property inquiries, qualifies buyers,
    schedules showings, syncs to CRM, sends follow-ups
  What the agency charges: $1,500-$2,000/month
  Pitch the agency gives their client: "Your AI handles every
    listing inquiry and books showings while you focus on closing."

  Industry: Home Services (HVAC, Plumbing, Roofing)
  AI Role: AI Dispatch Agent
  What it does: Answers service calls, qualifies urgency, books
    service windows, updates job board, sends technician notifications
  What the agency charges: $1,000-$2,000/month
  Pitch the agency gives their client: "Never miss a service call
    again. Your AI books jobs 24/7."

  Industry: Fitness / Gyms / Studios
  AI Role: AI Membership Advisor
  What it does: Answers prospect calls, explains membership options,
    books trial classes, follows up with no-shows, re-engages
    lapsed members
  What the agency charges: $1,000-$1,500/month
  Pitch the agency gives their client: "Your AI converts leads into
    members and brings back the ones who stopped showing up."

  Industry: Med Spas / Cosmetic
  AI Role: AI Consultation Booker
  What it does: Answers calls, explains services, books consultations,
    sends prep instructions, remembers client preferences
  What the agency charges: $1,500-$2,500/month
  Pitch the agency gives their client: "Your AI handles every inquiry
    and fills your consultation calendar without your front desk
    lifting a finger."

  Industry: Legal
  AI Role: AI Intake Specialist
  What it does: Answers calls, conducts initial intake questions,
    qualifies case type, schedules consultations, sends retainer info
  What the agency charges: $2,000-$3,000/month
  Pitch the agency gives their client: "Your AI qualifies and books
    every lead so your team only talks to real cases."


KEY POINT: Give the agency owner these pitches. They're not just
buying your build — they're buying a sales script for their own
clients. The easier you make it for them to sell, the more they sell,
the more you build.

9. THE PARTNERSHIP MODEL (STRUCTURE, PRICING, TERMS)
================================================================

THE WHITE-LABEL STRUCTURE

  How it works:
    1. Agency sells AI service to their end client (dental practice,
       real estate firm, etc.)
    2. Agency introduces us to the client's situation (what they need,
       what tools they use, what the workflow looks like)
    3. We build the AI system
    4. It goes live under the agency's brand — the end client sees the
       agency's name, not ours
    5. We maintain and monitor the system
    6. Agency handles the client relationship

  What the agency does: Sells. Manages client. Collects payment.
  What we do: Builds. Maintains. Monitors. Optimizes.


PRICING STRUCTURE

  There are two pricing components:

  1. Setup Fee (one-time):
     What it covers: Building the AI system, integrating with the
     client's tools, testing, going live
     Our wholesale price to the agency: $2,000-$4,000
     What the agency charges their client: $3,000-$7,000
     Agency margin: 30-50%

  2. Monthly Maintenance (recurring):
     What it covers: Monitoring, optimization, updates, support
     Our wholesale price to the agency: $300-$800/month
     What the agency charges their client: $1,000-$2,500/month
     Agency margin: 50-70%

  Why this works for the agency:
    - The setup fee covers their sales cost
    - The monthly maintenance is pure recurring revenue
    - Their margin is healthy (50%+ on recurring)
    - They're adding $12,000-$30,000/year per client in revenue
      they didn't have before

  Why this works for us:
    - The agency finds and sells the client — zero outbound cost
    - We focus on building — our core strength
    - Monthly maintenance creates recurring revenue for us too
    - One agency with 20 clients = potential for 3-5 AI projects/year


TERMS TO ESTABLISH UPFRONT

  Non-compete / exclusivity:
    - We will NOT go direct to any of their clients. Ever. Put it
      in writing if they need it. This is their #1 fear.
    - We do NOT grant exclusivity to any single agency. We can
      work with multiple agencies in the same market. But we never
      share information between agencies about each other's clients.

  Scope of work:
    - Define clearly what "the AI system" includes for each project
    - Define what's an add-on (new features, new workflows, major
      changes after go-live)
    - Avoid open-ended "unlimited revisions" — define 2 revision
      rounds in the setup phase

  Communication:
    - Agency is the point of contact for their client. We do not
      communicate directly with the end client unless the agency
      introduces us.
    - We provide the agency with updates, reports, and any issues.
    - The agency relays to their client.

  Payment:
    - Setup fee: 50% upfront, 50% on go-live
    - Monthly maintenance: invoiced monthly, due net 15
    - If the agency's client stops paying, the agency still pays us
      for that month (the agency manages collections, not us)

10. THE FIRST CALL: HOW TO RUN IT
================================================================

This is a 15-20 minute call. Not a sales pitch. A partnership
conversation between two business owners.


THE STRUCTURE

  Minutes 1-3: THEIR WORLD
    Ask, don't tell. Understand their business first.

    "Tell me about your agency — what industries do your clients
    come from?"

    "How many active clients are you managing right now?"

    "Are any of them asking about AI, or is this something you're
    proactively thinking about?"

    "Have you tried building any AI features for clients yet?
    What happened?"

    Listen. Take notes. You're learning what to customize in
    your pitch.


  Minutes 3-8: THE PRODUCT (THROUGH THEIR LENS)
    Now show, don't tell. Use their industry.

    "So you work with dental practices — let me show you exactly
    what we'd build for one of your dental clients."

    [Describe the AI Receptionist in 60 seconds — what it does,
    the action chain, 24/7 coverage, no missed calls]

    "Want to hear it? Call this number."

    [Let them call the demo. Shut up while they talk to the AI.
    The demo does the selling.]

    After the demo:
    "That's a live system we built. That's what your clients
    would get, under your brand."


  Minutes 8-12: THE BUSINESS MODEL
    Now show them the money.

    "Here's how the business model works for you.

    Setup: you charge your client $5,000 for the build.
    You pay us $3,000. You keep $2,000.

    Monthly: you charge $2,000/month for the AI service.
    You pay us $500/month. You keep $1,500/month.

    That's $18,000/year per client in net recurring revenue
    for you. We handle all the building and maintenance.

    If you add this to 5 of your clients, that's $90,000/year
    in new revenue without hiring anyone."

    Let the math sink in. Don't rush past this.


  Minutes 12-15: THE PILOT
    Close with a low-risk next step.

    "Here's what I'd suggest. Let's do one project together.
    Pick your best client — the one most likely to say yes to
    an AI add-on. We build it. You see the quality. If it
    works, we make this ongoing.

    Zero risk. If the first one doesn't land, no hard feelings.
    But I think once your client sees it working, you'll want
    to roll it out to more."


  Minutes 15-20: QUESTIONS
    Let them ask anything. Common questions:

    "What if my client hates it?" — We test extensively before
    go-live. And we optimize based on real call data for the
    first 30 days.

    "What if they want changes?" — 2 rounds of revision are

11. OBJECTION HANDLING
================================================================

Every objection an agency owner raises maps to a specific fear.
Address the fear, not the words.


"MY CLIENTS CAN'T AFFORD THIS"

  The fear: Their clients are small/local businesses with tight budgets.

  The response:
    "What's their cost of a missed call? If a dental practice
    misses 10 calls a week and each appointment is worth $300,
    that's $12,000/month in potential lost revenue. The AI costs
    $2,000/month. The ROI pitch writes itself.

    We can also start with a lighter version for price-sensitive
    clients — just the voice agent + booking. Then expand later."


"I CAN BUILD THIS MYSELF WITH GHL + VAPI"

  The fear: They don't want to pay for something they think they can do.

  The response:
    "Totally — and for basic use cases, GHL's built-in tools work
    fine. Where we come in is when you want production-grade:
    voice that handles real calls without breaking, memory that
    remembers returning callers, and deep CRM automation beyond
    what GHL's chatbot does natively.

    We also handle maintenance. When something breaks at 2am on
    a Saturday for your client, we're the ones who fix it.
    That alone is worth the partnership for most agency owners."


"WHAT IF THE AI EMBARRASSES MY CLIENT'S BRAND?"

  The fear: The AI says something wrong to a real customer.

  The response:
    "That's the #1 thing we optimize for. Every system goes through
    a testing phase with real scenarios before it touches a single
    customer. We build guardrails — things the AI is NOT allowed to
    say, fallback to human handoff when it's unsure, and we monitor
    the first 30 days of live calls to catch and fix edge cases.

    I can also show you the monitoring dashboard we set up for
    agency partners — you can see every call, every response, and
    flag anything that needs adjustment."


"I DON'T WANT YOU GOING TO MY CLIENTS DIRECTLY"

  The fear: You'll cut them out.

  The response:
    "100% — and I'll put that in writing. We never contact your
    clients directly. You own the client relationship. We're your
    backend build team, not your competition. If your client emails
    us, we forward it to you. Full stop.

    This only works if agencies trust us. We'd torch our own
    business model by going around you."


"I NEED TO THINK ABOUT IT"

  The fear: They're not sure it's worth the risk/effort.

  The response:
    "Totally fair. Here's what I'd suggest — pick one client.
    The easiest one. The one who's already asked about AI or who's
    already frustrated with missed calls. Let us build one system

    No long-term commitment. Just one project to test the fit."

    "In the meantime, share this number with anyone. Let them call

"YOUR PRICE IS TOO HIGH"

  The fear: They can't make the margin work.

  The response:

    If you're comparing to doing it yourself: how many hours
    would it take you to build, maintain, and troubleshoot a
    production voice agent? At your hourly rate, the math
    doesn't work for DIY."

12. THE PILOT PROJECT: HOW TO CLOSE AND DELIVER
================================================================

The pilot is everything. Nail it and the agency becomes a long-term
partner. Fumble it and you lose them forever.


CLOSING THE PILOT

  The ask:
    "Pick one client. The easiest sell. We build the AI system.
    You see the quality. Then we talk about scaling."

  Pricing for the pilot:
    Option 1: Full price, standard terms
      If the agency is confident and has immediate demand
    Option 2: Discounted setup, full monthly rate
      If the agency is hesitant — reduce the setup fee by 30-50%
      to lower the barrier. Keep the monthly rate standard because
      that's where recurring revenue lives.
    Option 3: "You only pay if the client pays"
      Last resort for high-potential agencies who are very hesitant.
      You build it, the agency sells it to their client, and you
      only invoice when the client pays. This eliminates 100% of
      risk for the agency. Only offer this if you're very confident
      in the agency's ability to sell.

  Never do the pilot for free. Even a discounted pilot establishes
  that your work has value. Free work attracts the wrong partners.


DELIVERING THE PILOT

  Week 1: Discovery
    - Get access to the agency's client's GHL account
    - 30-minute call with the agency (and optionally the end client)
      to understand the business, the workflow, the FAQs, the
      services, the edge cases
    - Document everything: what the AI should say, what it should
      never say, what actions it should take, what the handoff to
      human looks like

  Week 2: Build
    - Build the voice agent, memory layer, and automation workflows
    - Internal testing with real scenarios
    - Share a demo with the agency for their review
    - Incorporate feedback (revision round 1)

  Week 3: Test + Go Live
    - Live testing with controlled real calls (agency owner calls,
      their team calls, maybe 1-2 real customers in a limited window)
    - Fix edge cases
    - Go live
    - Monitor every call for the first 7 days

  Week 4: Optimize + Report
    - Review first week of live data
    - Optimize responses, fix any issues
    - Send the agency a simple report:
      Calls handled: [X]
      Appointments booked: [X]
      Issues flagged: [X]
      Client satisfaction: [qualitative]
    - Use this report to have the "scale" conversation:
      "This is working. Which of your other clients should we
      build this for next?"

13. SCALING FROM 1 AGENCY TO 5
================================================================

THE FIRST AGENCY TEACHES YOU EVERYTHING

  Your first agency partnership is not about revenue. It's about:
    - Learning how agencies sell AI to their clients
    - Learning what the end clients actually need
    - Building your first case study / proof asset
    - Refining your delivery process
    - Understanding the agency's communication needs

  After the first successful project with your first agency:
    - Ask them for a testimonial
    - Ask them to introduce you to other agency owners
    - Create a case study: "[Agency's industry] agency added $X/month
      in recurring revenue with white-label AI. Here's how."


THE REFERRAL PLAY

  Happy agency owners talk to other agency owners. GHL groups are
  tight communities. If you deliver great work, your agency partner
  will mention you in groups, recommend you in threads, and introduce
  you directly.

  Accelerate this:
    - After a successful pilot, ask: "Do you know 1-2 other agency
      owners who'd benefit from this?" (Direct referral)
    - After 2-3 successful projects, ask: "Would you mind posting
      about the results in your GHL group?" (Public social proof)
    - Offer a referral bonus: $500 for every agency they introduce
      that signs a project (Incentive)


THE SCALING TIMELINE

  Month 1-2: 1 agency partner, 1 pilot project
    Focus: Learn, deliver, refine

  Month 3-4: 2-3 agency partners, 3-5 active projects
    Focus: Repeatable delivery, templated workflows

  Month 5-6: 4-5 agency partners, 8-12 active projects
    Focus: Systems for scale — onboarding templates, monitoring
    dashboards, standardized reporting

  By month 6: You should have a repeatable machine where agencies
  find clients, sell AI services, and you build/maintain the systems.


WHAT TO SYSTEMATIZE AS YOU SCALE

  1. Onboarding template
     A standard document the agency fills out for each new client:
     business info, services, FAQs, tools, workflow, edge cases.

  2. Build templates
     Reusable system architectures per industry. The dental AI
     Receptionist template, the real estate Showing Coordinator
     template, etc. Customize per client, but start from 80%
     done.

  3. Monitoring dashboard
     One place where you (and optionally the agency) can see
     all active AI systems, call volumes, issues, performance.

  4. Monthly report template
     Standard report format you send agencies for each client:
     calls handled, appointments booked, issues, optimizations made.

  5. Agency partner portal (eventually)
     A simple page where agency partners can see their clients'
     AI systems, performance, and submit new client requests.

15. RED FLAGS: AGENCIES TO WALK AWAY FROM
================================================================

Not every agency is a good partner. Walk away from these:

  "Can you build it for free and we'll pay you from the first client?"
    Translation: They don't have money and don't value your work.
    Even a discounted pilot is fine. Free is not.

  "We just need a basic chatbot, nothing fancy"
    Translation: They want a $200 chatbot, not a $3,000 AI system.
    They'll push back on every price point.

  "We already have 3 AI vendors we're evaluating"
    Translation: They're shopping for the cheapest option.
    They'll commoditize you. Pass unless you can clearly
    differentiate on the call.

  "Our clients have zero budget for add-ons"
    Translation: Their clients are too small or too cheap for AI.
    You can't force a market that isn't ready.

  "Can we get the source code / full ownership?"
    Translation: They want to learn how you build it and then do
    it themselves. Share access to the running system, never the
    underlying architecture.

  "We need this done by next week"
    Translation: They've overpromised to a client and need you
    to bail them out. One emergency is fine. If it's a pattern,
    they'll burn you out.

  Agency has fewer than 5 clients and no growth trajectory:
    They won't generate enough volume to make the partnership
    worth your time. Be friendly, point them to DIY resources.

  Agency owner is combative or dismissive in the DMs:
    If they're difficult before money is involved, they'll be
    impossible after. Trust the signal.
